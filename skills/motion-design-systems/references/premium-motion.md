# Premium Motion Reference

Use this for high-end product reveal, launch video, and enterprise demo motion.

## Works Well

- Slow camera drift to create continuity across scenes.
- Slight parallax between foreground UI and background glass.
- Material emergence: scale, blur, refraction, highlight sweep, shadow formation.
- Path tracing for charts, rings, connectors, and outlines.
- Micro shimmer on glass surfaces after the main animation settles.
- Hold frames that keep ambient motion alive without distracting from narration.

## Usually Fails

- Bounce or rebound after a stomp unless explicitly requested.
- Opacity-only reveals for elements described as physical, glass, drawn, traced, or forming.
- Dense panels that look like generic SaaS dashboards.
- Overly dark, neon, sci-fi, or HUD-like interfaces for premium enterprise videos.
- Big animated decorations that do not communicate the narrative.

## Timing Heuristics

- Give important text enough time to land before the narration moves on.
- Use exact timestamps for narrative reveals.
- If a scene must hold after narration, use a real hold or subtle ambient loop, not frozen dead stillness.
- For final frames, arrive early enough for proof and comprehension.
